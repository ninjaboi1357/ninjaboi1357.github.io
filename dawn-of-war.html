Dawn of War

In 1937, during the midst of the Second Sino-Japanese War, Japanese aircraft attacked the USS Panay, an American gunboat, killing three Americans. The Japanese refusal to take responsibility for the attack caused heightened tensions between the two powers, hinting that the two Pacific powers could not have coexisted.



In 1939, the Danzig Crisis in Poland brought about the start of the Second World War in Europe. Emboldened by the signing of the Tripartite Pact in 1940, a defensive military alliance between Germany, Italy, and Japan, Japan furthered its aggression in the Pacific. In retaliation, America froze all Japanese foreign assets and embargoed oil exports to Japan.



Aware of its limited resources, the Japanese Empire decided that a decisive attack was required to destroy the American Navy before those resources ran out. With diplomacy failing in 1941, a surprise attack on Pearl Harbor was launched by Japan, waking the "sleeping giant."
